Assignment 1 - Public Transport in Hartford

Hartford is the capitol of Connecticut and is geographically centered in the state. Therefore, it's pubic transportation systems are also central to statewide systems of similar type, whether that means that the Hartfield transit system is the physical hub of the network, or is the template. CTtransit is the Connecticut Department of Transportation (CTDOT)-owned bus service. Several companies under contract to CTDOT operate services in metropolitan areas throughout Connecticut, but the focus here is one the central system in the Hartford area. CTtransit Hartford is the hub of the other state-subsidized systems, and therefore it's reach is beyond that which is shown below. However, it's use is a reflection of the statewide system, therefore it is worth looking into.

Mapping of Hartford's Bus System

Mapped below are the stops directly under the CTtransit Hartford group. Variation in opacity shows number of routes that use each station.

CTtransit use

The span of CTtransit Hartford displays a very strong north-south bearing in it's direct design, which hints at division of authority. It seems that the state-mandated system is only initialized for the use of connection to localized express networks that do run east-west, as well as provide access to longer reaching transit methods such as CTrail, Connecticut's passender railway service, that itself runs north-south. Additionally, CTtransit's CTfastrak bus extension that connects the Hartford branch to the New Haven branch seems to make it so consolidation of routes along this axis is preferable.
